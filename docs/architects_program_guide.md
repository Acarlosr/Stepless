## What is the Architects Program?

The **Arc Architects Program** is Circle's ambassador program for developers building on Arc — the stablecoin-native L1. It rewards contributors with points, escalating tiers, and recognition for building, teaching, and growing the Arc ecosystem.

Think of it as a structured way to:

- **Get recognized** for your Arc contributions (not just code — content, community, demos)
- **Earn points** that translate into tiers with real benefits
- **Access Circle DevRel** directly through office hours and private channels
- **Position yourself** for airdrops, grants, and ecosystem recognition

The program is run through **Arc House**, the Discord community for Arc builders.

---

## How to Join

### 1. Join Arc House

Arc House is the Discord community for the Arc ecosystem. This is where the Architects Program lives.

- **Discord:** [discord.gg/archouse](https://discord.gg/archouse)
- **Request the Architect role** in the `#roles` channel
- **Introduce yourself** in `#introductions` — mention you're building Stepless

### 2. Start Contributing

Once you're in Arc House, start contributing in any of these categories:

- Build projects on Arc (Stepless counts!)
- Write tutorials and content about Arc
- Help other builders in Discord
- Participate in hackathons
- Share demos and get feedback

### 3. Track Your Points

Points are tracked through the Architects Program system. Check the `#architects` channel in Arc House for the current leaderboard and point-claiming process.

## Roles and Responsibilities

As you progress through tiers, you take on more responsibility in the Arc community.

### Apprentice (Tier 1)

- Learn the Arc ecosystem (RPC, USDC, Gas Station, Crossmint)
- Join Arc House and introduce yourself
- Start building or contributing to projects
- Ask questions and learn from others

### Builder (Tier 2)

- Ship at least one project on Arc (Stepless deployment counts!)
- Help newcomers in Discord with basic questions
- Share your demos and get feedback
- Write your first tutorial or content piece

### Architect (Tier 3)

- Consistently contribute high-quality content and code
- Mentor Builders and Apprentices
- Participate in hackathons representing Arc
- Provide feedback to Circle DevRel on Arc features
- Host a demo or workshop in Arc House

### Master Architect (Tier 4)

- Lead community initiatives
- Represent Arc at external events and hackathons
- Contribute to Arc documentation and tooling
- Provide strategic feedback to Circle product team
- Help review and onboard new Architects

### Grand Architect (Tier 5)

- Serve as a community leader and spokesperson
- Speak at Arc events and conferences
- Mentor Master Architects
- Influence Arc roadmap through direct product feedback
- Help shape the Architects Program itself

## Office Hours

Circle's Developer Relations team holds **bi-weekly office hours** exclusively for Architects Program members.

### What to Bring

- Technical questions about Arc (RPC, USDC, Gas Station, Crossmint)
- Deployment issues or debugging help
- Feedback on Arc developer experience
- Project demos and progress updates
- Questions about grants, hackathons, or ecosystem opportunities

### Schedule

- **Frequency:** Bi-weekly (every other week)
- **Format:** Video call (link posted in `#architects` channel)
- **Duration:** 60 minutes
- **Recording:** Posted for async viewing if you can't attend live

### How to Prepare

1. Post your question in `#architects` before office hours
2. Circle DevRel will prioritize questions posted in advance
3. Have your code or demo ready to screen-share
4. Take notes and share learnings with the community afterward

## Arc Engagement Amplification Guide

Content you create about Arc and Stepless amplifies both the ecosystem and your Architects Program standing. Follow these guidelines to maximize impact.

### Content Principles

1. **Be specific about Arc** — Don't just say "blockchain." Talk about USDC-native L1, sub-cent fees, 3-second finality, Gas Station, Crossmint.
2. **Show, don't tell** — Include code snippets, screenshots, tx hashes on ArcScan, live demos.
3. **Teach what you learned** — Tutorials and "how I built X" posts earn more points than announcements.
4. **Tag appropriately** — Use `@ArcNetwork`, `@CircleDevs`, relevant hashtags (#Arc #USDC #Stablecoin #Accessibility)
5. **Cross-post** — Share in Arc House Discord, Twitter/X, and relevant subreddits/communities

### Content Formats

| Format | Platform | Points Potential | Tips |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tutorial blog post** | Medium, Mirror, dev.to | 100–150 | Include full code, deploy steps, ArcScan links |
| **Twitter/X thread** | Twitter/X | 10–50 | 10+ tweets, screenshots, code snippets, CTA to repo |
| **YouTube video** | YouTube | 50–200 | Screen recording of deploy + demo, 5–15 min |
| **Discord demo** | Arc House | 10–30 | Screen share in `#demos`, share tx hashes |
| **Hackathon submission** | Event platform | 50–500 | Deploy live, include demo video, link to ArcScan |
| **Workshop** | Arc House / event | 50–150 | Live coding session, 30–60 min, Q&A |

### Content Ideas for Stepless + Arc

- "How to deploy a USDC treasury contract on Arc Testnet"
- "Building accessibility infrastructure with stablecoin micropayments"
- "Arc Gas Station: sponsoring transactions for non-crypto users"
- "Using Crossmint to onboard contributors without crypto"
- "Indexing Arc events with Goldsky subgraphs"
- "Foundry + Arc: testing stablecoin contracts with 6-decimal USDC"
- "From idea to mainnet: deploying Stepless on Arc"

### Amplification Checklist

- ☐ Post in Arc House `#demos` or `#content` channel
- ☐ Tag `@ArcNetwork` and `@CircleDevs` on Twitter/X
- ☐ Use hashtags: `#Arc #USDC #Stablecoin #Accessibility #Web3`
- ☐ Link to ArcScan tx hashes as proof of deployment
- ☐ Link to the Stepless GitHub repo
- ☐ Share in relevant accessibility communities (cross-pollination)
- ☐ Ask for feedback in Arc House
- ☐ Submit for Architects Program points

## Contribution Opportunities for Stepless

Stepless is an ideal Architects Program flagship project because it combines technical depth (3 smart contracts, subgraph, mobile app) with social impact (accessibility). Here are specific ways to contribute:

### 1. Write About Accessibility + Blockchain

The intersection of accessibility and Web3 is largely unexplored. Your content will stand out.

**Topics:**
- "Why accessibility infrastructure needs programmable money"
- "How USDC micropayments on Arc can fund accessibility mapping"
- "Decentralizing accessibility data: from volunteers to paid contributors"
- "WCAG compliance as an on-chain verifiable standard"

**Where to publish:** Medium, Mirror, dev.to, Arc House blog, accessibility forums

**Points:** 80–150 per post

### 2. Share Stepless Demos in Arc House

Regularly share your progress in the `#demos` channel:

- Deploy Stepless to Arc Testnet → share ArcScan links
- Submit a real accessibility location → show the tx
- Claim a reward → show the USDC transfer
- Deploy the subgraph → share the GraphQL query results
- Launch the frontend → share the URL

**Points:** 10–30 per demo

### 3. Create Tutorials About Arc-Specific Development

Arc has unique properties that aren't well-documented yet. Your tutorials fill this gap:

- "Handling USDC's 6 decimals in Solidity (common pitfalls)"
- "Using the Circle Gas Station to sponsor user transactions"
- "Integrating Crossmint for fiat-to-USDC on-ramping"
- "Deploying a Goldsky subgraph for Arc events"
- "Two-phase contract deployment on Arc with Foundry"
- "Testing Arc contracts with forge fork tests"

**Points:** 100–150 per tutorial

### 4. Participate in Hackathons

Enter Arc-sponsored hackathons with Stepless:

- Deploy a polished version to Arc Testnet
- Prepare a 3-minute demo video
- Include ArcScan links in your submission
- Highlight the social impact angle (accessibility)
- Mention the Architects Program in your bio

**Points:** 50–200 for participation, 200–500 for winning

### 5. Help Other Builders with Arc Questions

Be active in Arc House Discord:

- Answer questions about Foundry + Arc
- Help with USDC decimal issues (the most common Arc bug)
- Guide new builders through faucet → deploy → verify flow
- Review other projects' smart contracts
- Share your deployment guide

**Points:** 5–20 per helpful answer, 50–100 for consistent help

### 6. Contribute Code to Stepless

Direct code contributions also count:

- Add fuzz tests and invariant tests
- Implement batch claim functionality
- Add new language translations (FR, DE, JP)
- Improve mobile app offline sync
- Optimize gas costs in hot paths
- Add WCAG 2.1 AA compliance improvements

**Points:** 20–100 per merged PR (depending on complexity)
